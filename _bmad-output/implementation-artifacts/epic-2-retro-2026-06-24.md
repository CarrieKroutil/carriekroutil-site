## What shipped

- **2.1** Post content model + front-matter contract + auto read-time (`.ReadingTime`, "{n} min read", floor 1); missing/invalid date **fails the build**.
- **2.2** Single post page — title, date, read-time, clickable tags, prose/code/alt-texted images, way back to the stream; drafts excluded.
- **2.3** Shared `post-card` partial — one renderer reused everywhere.
- **2.4** Full reverse-chron post stream at `/posts/` (no client-side filtering).

## What went well

- **The build gate works and was proven to work.** Missing-date → build failure was fault-injected and confirmed during review, not just asserted. This is the right standard for "build-enforced" requirements.
- **One card renderer, no duplication.** The shared `post-card.html` partial built here became the reuse anchor for Epics 3 and 4 — exactly as the epic plan intended ("reuses the Epic 2 card partial; no new card markup").
- **Accessible overlay card** resolved the UX-DR5-vs-FR-4-AC2 tension (whole-card → post, tag chip → term page) without nested interactive elements.
- **Tasteful per-topic chip tinting** via `hash.FNV32a` — deterministic color per tag with no maintenance.

## What was hard / what evolved

- **Routing approach changed after the build notes were written.** The mid-epic note recorded a `cascade type:blog` + `layouts/blog/` approach; the shipped (and durable) design is **section-name routing** under `content/posts/` with `layouts/posts/{single,list}.html` and **no `type: blog` cascade**. The simpler section-based approach won. Lesson: build-time notes capture intent in flight; the merged code is the source of truth.
- **Chroma code-block background** (`#272822` monokai) is off-palette but functional — left as a future polish item, still open.

## Lessons

1. **Fault-inject your gates.** "Build fails on missing date" is only credible once you've seen it fail. Carry this to every build-enforced rule.
2. **Build one renderer, reuse it.** The single post-card partial paid compounding dividends across three later epics. Resist per-page card variants.
3. **Trust the merged code over in-flight notes** when they disagree.
