## What shipped

- **5.1** Discoverability — sitemap + indexing `robots.txt` (absolute `Sitemap:`), descriptive `<title>`/meta description, OG/Twitter tags reading `hero.src`/`hero.alt` via a single `custom/social-image.html` feeding the head-end hook + a `twitter_cards.html` override (→ `summary_large_image`). Site avatar is the default share image; new site-level `params.description` covers heroless/home/about pages.
- **5.2** BMAD build-in-public launch post — wins the featured slot (12:00 date beats hello's 09:00); "hello" moves to the recent grid.
- **5.3** Privacy-friendly analytics — **GoatCounter** via `custom/analytics.html`, gated twice (`hugo.IsProduction` + `with .Site.Params.goatcounter`); prod-only, no cookies, no PII (NFR-3). Verified: prod build emits on all 13 pages; dev build emits 0.
- **5.4** On-brand custom 404 (`layouts/404.html`, define `main` → Hextra chrome), reusing `.ck-btn`; visually verified.

## What went well

- **Double-gated analytics is the right privacy posture.** Prod-only (local previews never count, AR-4) *and* fork-safe (`with .Site.Params.goatcounter`), script-include only, no consent banner. Verified emit-on-prod / zero-on-dev rather than assumed.
- **Tool choice was deferred until it mattered, then made well.** GoatCounter over Cloudflare because Cloudflare's zero-JS edge advantage is lost on Amplify and GoatCounter's per-page dashboard fits a blog. A homegrown DynamoDB+Lambda counter was considered and explicitly rejected (reinvents bot-dedup for no benefit Carrie wanted).
- **One social-image partial** solved OG/Twitter image+description across post / heroless / home / about page types without scattering logic.

## What was hard / known limitations

- **SVG hero share images.** Heroes are SVG; some scrapers (older X) don't preview SVG `og:image`. This is a pre-existing site-wide pattern — the AC of *emitting* an image is met. Flagged for Carrie; raster fallback would be a future enhancement, not a launch blocker.

## Lessons

1. **Defer reversible tool choices until you have the constraints.** Waiting on the GoatCounter-vs-Cloudflare decision until the Amplify hosting reality was known produced a better choice than guessing during planning.
2. **Verify privacy gates empirically.** "Prod-only, no cookies" was confirmed by diffing prod vs dev HTML output, not asserted.
3. **Reject the gold-plated option out loud.** Writing down *why* the DynamoDB+Lambda counter was rejected is as valuable as the choice made — it stops the question from recurring.
