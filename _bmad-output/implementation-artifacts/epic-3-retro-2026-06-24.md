## What shipped

- **3.2** Latest-posts preview grid (4–6 newest as full-card link targets), reusing the Epic 2 `post-card`.

- **3.4** About page (`layout: about`) with bio, topic chips, GitHub + LinkedIn links.

## What went well

- **Chips driven from the live taxonomy.** Hero and About topic chips come from the actual `tags` taxonomy (`ByCount`), so every chip links a real `/tags/{topic}/` page — no broken links, no hand-maintained list. This is the standout decision of the epic.
- **Zero new card markup.** The featured slot and grid both reuse the Epic 2 partial — the planned reuse held perfectly.
- **Graceful avatar fallback.** Gradient-ring with `params.avatar` photo *or* an on-brand monogram, so the page looks intentional even before Carrie drops a real photo.
- **False-positive discipline.** The two review findings were chased down in rendered HTML + screenshots before being dismissed — not waved off.

## What was hard / what evolved

- Two convention cleanups surfaced in review (extracting a shared `custom/topic-terms.html` used by both hero and About; consolidating the gradient text-clip onto `.ck-grad-text`). Both were real DRY improvements — a sign the first pass had minor duplication between the hero and About chip rendering.

## Lessons

1. **Derive UI from data you already have.** Pulling chips from the live taxonomy eliminated an entire class of broken-link/maintenance bugs. Prefer derived over hand-maintained lists everywhere.
2. **Verify before dismissing review findings.** Screenshots + rendered HTML turned "is this a bug?" into a definitive answer.
3. **Watch for hero/About duplication.** The two pages share chrome; factor shared partials early next time.
