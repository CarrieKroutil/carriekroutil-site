There's a moment every morning, somewhere between the first coffee and the first meeting,
where three sets of paws start orbiting me like I'm the sun. No agenda. No inbox. Just a deep,
unshakable belief that the best thing that could possibly happen next is a walk.

And the thing is — they're right.

I spend most of my day in the land of fuzzy ideas and hard problems, which I genuinely love.
But my dogs operate on a much simpler spec: _be here, go outside, love the people, repeat._
No backlog. No roadmap. Just the next good thing, taken at full speed.

So we walk. Same loop, mostly. They sniff the same three fence posts like breaking news,
and I let my brain go quiet for twenty minutes. I come back better every time — clearer head,
looser shoulders, a problem that somehow untangled itself while I wasn't looking.

People talk about morning routines like they need an app. Mine has fur and bad recall and
zero chill, and I wouldn't trade it.

More dog stories on the way — they give me plenty of material. 🐾
