Management runs on a quiet assumption that you'll absorb whatever the team and the org can't — and if you let it, that absorption never stops. Boundaries aren't selfish; they're what makes you a leader people can rely on for the long haul instead of one who flames out in eighteen months. This topic will cover setting sustainable limits without guilt — much of which starts with how you run your [time and energy]({{< relref "/handbook/managing-yourself/time-energy-management.md" >}}) — modeling those limits so your team feels permission to do the same, and recognizing the early signs of burnout in yourself — the cynicism, the dread, the numbness — long before they become a crisis. For now, the Go Deeper resources are the best place to start.
